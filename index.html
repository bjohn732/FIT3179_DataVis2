Chilling With Statistics

As places are slowly recovering from the surge of COVID-19 with the increasing availability and varieties of vaccines,
Netflix, one of the streaming platform giants, continues to keep us entertained with their endless varieties of award-winning films & TV shows.
This visualisation takes a closer look at some of Netflix's recent data around the world.

Academy Awards (Oscar)
- celebrating film professionals and excellence

With a history dating back to the year 1929 where the first Oscar was given, it is the oldest among all academy awards. Among the famous theories of how the award's nickname came to be include one which states that Director of the Academy, Margaret Herrick, took a glance at the statuette and noted that it resembled her Uncle Oscar

The choropleth map on the right summarizes each country's Netflix catalog based on the number of Oscar-winning film titles. Feel free to hover over the marks for more information.



Television Academy Awards (Emmy) - celebrating TV professionals and excellence

This award is dedicated to TV shows or programs made either on Daytime or Primetime. The origin of its nickname "Emmy" is a term known as "immy" which was used for the image orthicon tube commonly found in early TV cameras.

The choropleth map on the left summarizes each country's Netflix catalog based on the number of Emmy-winning TV Show titles. Feel free to hover over the marks for more information.

How did your country's Netflix library do compared to the rest of the world? To view a larger scope of things, we now take a look at trending genre titles for each continent. This data was taken from flixpatrol.com for titles trending in weeks 12-37 of the year 2020.

Different Place, Different Taste

The stacked bar chart on the right shows the trending title genres for each continent.

At first glance, we can immediately see that North America has the most trending titles in the specified genres. Next up, by a significant difference in terms of trending titles, would be Europe.

Africa and South America seem to have the least trending titles for the specified genres. This could be due to the respective communities having different tastes in films and shows.

A notable observation would be that in Asia, dramas are more favoured; this is especially true due to the Korean wave taking the world by storm. Hot-topics' of the year 2020 include
'It's Okay to Not Be Okay', 'Itaewon Class' and 'Crash Landing On You'.

Boosted by the pandemic, Netflix's total subscriptions has grown exponentially especially in the first half of 2020, which was during the outbreak.
